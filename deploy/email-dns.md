# Email DNS — SPF, DKIM, DMARC

Operator runbook for getting QuizOnline's outbound mail to land in
recipients' inboxes rather than spam. All three records live on the
**sending domain** (the part after the `@` in `DEFAULT_FROM_EMAIL`),
not on the website domain.

Replace `foxugly.com` below with your actual sending domain.

## 1. SPF — authorise your sender

A single `TXT` record at the apex of the sending domain that lists
every host or service allowed to send mail "From: anything@foxugly.com".

### If you send via Office 365 / Microsoft 365 (current default)

```
foxugly.com.   TXT  "v=spf1 include:spf.protection.outlook.com -all"
```

### If you also send via the local SMTP relay on EC2

```
foxugly.com.   TXT  "v=spf1 include:spf.protection.outlook.com ip4:YOUR.EC2.IPV4 -all"
```

### If you switch to a transactional provider (SendGrid / Postmark / Mailgun)

Replace the `include:` with the provider's documented value, e.g.:

```
foxugly.com.   TXT  "v=spf1 include:sendgrid.net -all"
```

Notes:

- Only one `v=spf1` TXT record is permitted per domain. Multiple records
  silently break SPF — merge providers into one record with multiple
  `include:` entries.
- The terminal `-all` enforces a hard fail for unauthorised senders.
  Start with `~all` (soft fail) while you verify, then tighten to `-all`.
- Verify with: `dig +short TXT foxugly.com` and any SPF checker.

## 2. DKIM — sign every outgoing message

The signature is generated by the provider; you publish their public key
on a sub-selector of your domain.

### Office 365

In the Microsoft 365 admin centre, generate two DKIM selectors and
publish the two `CNAME` records they hand you, typically:

```
selector1._domainkey.foxugly.com.  CNAME  selector1-foxugly-com._domainkey.<tenant>.onmicrosoft.com.
selector2._domainkey.foxugly.com.  CNAME  selector2-foxugly-com._domainkey.<tenant>.onmicrosoft.com.
```

Then go back to the admin centre and toggle **Sign messages for this
domain with DKIM** to ON. DNS propagation must finish first or the
toggle fails.

### Transactional provider

Most providers walk you through it in their dashboard — they generate
the `CNAME` targets, you paste them into DNS, then click "verify" in
their UI.

Verify with: `dig +short CNAME selector1._domainkey.foxugly.com`.

## 3. DMARC — tell receivers what to do with unauthenticated mail

A single `TXT` record at `_dmarc.foxugly.com` that:
- declares your policy when SPF or DKIM fails,
- asks Gmail / Outlook to e-mail you aggregate failure reports.

### Phase 1 — monitor only (start here)

```
_dmarc.foxugly.com.   TXT  "v=DMARC1; p=none; rua=mailto:dmarc-reports@foxugly.com; aspf=s; adkim=s; pct=100"
```

- `p=none` — receivers still deliver the mail, just send you reports.
- `rua=` — XML aggregate reports land at this address (1/day per
  receiver). Set up a real inbox; XML is unfriendly but you only need
  to check it weekly.
- `aspf=s; adkim=s` — strict alignment: the visible `From:` domain must
  exactly match the SPF-checked / DKIM-signing domain. Loosen to `r`
  (relaxed) only if a subdomain sends on your behalf.

Run with `p=none` for **at least two weeks** until reports show 100%
SPF + DKIM pass. Anything you can't authenticate at this point is
either misconfigured or a spammer impersonating you — fix the first,
ignore the second.

### Phase 2 — quarantine

```
_dmarc.foxugly.com.   TXT  "v=DMARC1; p=quarantine; pct=25; rua=mailto:dmarc-reports@foxugly.com; aspf=s; adkim=s"
```

- `p=quarantine; pct=25` — Gmail/Outlook silently send 25% of failing
  mail to spam. Use this for a week and watch the reports.

### Phase 3 — reject

```
_dmarc.foxugly.com.   TXT  "v=DMARC1; p=reject; rua=mailto:dmarc-reports@foxugly.com; aspf=s; adkim=s"
```

- `p=reject` — receivers refuse failing mail at SMTP time. Only flip
  here when Phase 2 reports show zero legitimate failures.

## 4. Verify the whole chain

After every step, send a test mail (e.g. via the in-app /admin "Test
e-mail" view) to `check-auth@verifier.port25.com` — it replies with a
plain-text report showing SPF pass/fail, DKIM signature validation and
DMARC alignment in one go. Or simply view source on a test mail in
Gmail and check the `Authentication-Results:` header reads
`spf=pass dkim=pass dmarc=pass`.

## 5. Operational tips

- **DNS TTL**: drop SPF/DMARC TTL to 300s before each change, restore
  to 3600s once stable. Lets you roll back in five minutes if anything
  breaks.
- **MTA-STS / TLS-RPT**: a level up from this runbook — worth adding
  once Phase 3 DMARC is stable.
- **Subdomain policy**: if you ever start sending from
  `noreply.foxugly.com`, add `sp=...` to the DMARC record to control
  subdomain behaviour separately.
- **BIMI**: optional logo in the recipient's inbox, only available
  once DMARC `p=reject` is enforced. Nice-to-have, not a deliverability
  blocker.
